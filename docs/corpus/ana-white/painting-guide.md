# Painting Guide

Source: Ana White

## 6-Step Process

### 1. Fill Holes

Apply wood filler (paintable type). Overfill slightly, let dry completely, and reapply if the filler shrinks below the surface.

### 2. Sand

Use medium grit sandpaper. Sand with the grain. Smooth out filler patches and rough edges, especially at joints.

### 3. Clean

Vacuum dust off, brush with a soft brush, then wipe with a damp lint-free cloth.

### 4. Prime

Apply a light, even coat of primer for bare wood. Use a foam roller for flat surfaces and a brush for corners. Start on inside surfaces and work outward.

### 5. Paint

Use a foam roller for large areas. Use a foam applicator or brush for corners and detail work. Sand lightly between coats.

### 6. Build Color

Repeat coats until coverage is solid and even. Expect 3-4 coats for full coverage.

## Recommended Products

- Wood filler (paintable)
- Medium and fine grit sandpaper
- Primer for bare wood
- Paint for trim/doors/cabinets (satin, semi-gloss, or gloss)
- Foam applicator and foam roller with tray

## Tips

- Choose a paint color that works with the room.
- Painted projects need extra precision -- all joints must line up cleanly since paint reveals gaps.
- Follow the manufacturer's directions on dry times and application.
- Always use primer on bare wood.
- When in doubt, use trim paint (it levels well and produces a durable finish).

## Finishing Terminology

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Water-based stain | Less toxic, easier cleanup with water. |
| Oil-based stain | Most common type. Use disposable applicators. |
| Polyurethane | Clear protective coat applied over stain. Use on tabletops and high-use surfaces. |
| Wood filler | Sawdust-and-glue mixture. Will not stain the same as raw wood -- always test on scrap first. |

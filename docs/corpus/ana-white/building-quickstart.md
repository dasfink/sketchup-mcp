# Building Quickstart Guide

Source: Ana White

## Safety First

- Always wear safety glasses and ear protection.
- Read tool manuals before use.
- Practice cuts on scrap wood.
- Keep cords, scraps, and clutter clear of your work area.

## Set Up Your Workspace

- Use a flat, sturdy surface: a workbench, plywood on sawhorses, or a level floor.
- A smooth 3/4" plywood top that is easy to clamp to works well.
- Ensure good lighting and ventilation.
- Keep tools organized and within reach.

## Essential Starter Tools

- Tape measure
- Speed square
- Clamps
- Pencil
- Drill/driver with bits
- Circular saw

Start with this basic set and grow your toolset over time.

## Before You Build

1. Pick a plan from a reputable source.
2. Study the plan thoroughly.
3. Watch build videos and read comments from other builders.
4. Visualize the full assembly sequence in your head.
5. Lay out all tools, hardware, and lumber before starting.

## While You Build

- Check for square often.
- Clamp pieces before fastening.
- Go slow -- accuracy is faster than fixing mistakes.

## After You Build

- Sand and finish safely (wear a dust mask, ensure ventilation).
- Clean up your workspace.

> "Every builder makes mistakes -- what matters is learning, adjusting, and enjoying the process."
